Mike Hernandez

After serving the neighborhoods and cities in and around Los Angeles for more than 23 years as Councilmember, Chief of Staff, Board Member and staff member, Mike Hernandez founded his own consulting firm in October 2013. Hernandez is the owner and Chief Strategist.

Hernandez was elected three times as Councilmember representing the First District on the Los Angeles City Council (1991-2001) and later worked for Councilmember Nate Holden for two years. Hernandez was also assistant Chief of Staff to Councilmember Bernard Parks and Councilmember Jan Perry for ten years.

Hernandez was involved and oversaw more than 23 city budgets, wrote and passed ballot initiatives-- Prop K (Parks), Prop D (Libraries) and Prop H (Olvera Street)-- and organized community members to lead the preservation and creation of open space in the park-poor neighborhoods inside the First District. As councilmember, Hernandez wrote the initial Taylor Yard Plan which became the framework for the development of what is now Rio de Los Angeles State Park, Sotomayor Learning Academies and the Los Angeles River Revitalization Plan.

As Chairman and Board Member of the Pasadena Gold Line Authority, Hernandez led the fight to bring the Gold Line through Northeast Los Angeles. His leadership made sure stations were added in Cypress Park, Southwest Museum, Lincoln Heights and Chinatown. Hernandez also served as Chair of the Ad Hoc Committee for Staples Center and began entitlement process for LA Live.

As a longtime resident of Cypress Park and local leader in Latino politics, his purpose as an elected official, representative of the City of Los Angeles, and now consultant, is to get solutions that make the community stronger. That mission has not strayed and will not because there is always work to be done to improve the quality of life for all residents in Los Angeles.
